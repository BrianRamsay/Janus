<p><b>Freeware</b> (from "free" and "software") is <a href="/wiki/Computer_software" title="Computer software">computer software</a> that is available for use at no cost (or for an optional fee).<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0"><span>[</span>1<span>]</span></a></sup> This is in contrast to <a href="/wiki/Commercial_software" title="Commercial software">commercial software</a>, which is typically sold for profit. Though "freeware" does not preclude <a href="/wiki/Free_and_open_source_software" title="Free and open source software">free and open source software</a> (FOSS), it is commonly used to denote cost-free <a href="/wiki/Proprietary_software" title="Proprietary software">proprietary software</a> in contrast to FOSS.</p>

<p>The term <i>freeware</i> was coined by <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Fluegelman" title="Andrew Fluegelman">Andrew Fluegelman</a> when he wanted to sell a communications program named <a href="/wiki/PC-Talk" title="PC-Talk">PC-Talk</a> that he had created but for which he did not wish to use traditional methods of distribution because of their cost.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1"><span>[</span>2<span>]</span></a></sup> Fluegelman actually distributed PC-Talk via a process now referred to as <a href="/wiki/Shareware" title="Shareware">shareware</a>. Current use of the term freeware does not necessarily match the original concept by Andrew Fluegelman.</p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="/w/index.php?title=Freeware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Criteria">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline" id="Criteria">Criteria</span></h2>
<p>Software classified as freeware is either fully functional for an unlimited time with no cost, monetary or otherwise; or has basic functions enabled at no cost, with a fully functional version available as commercial or shareware. Freeware can be <a href="/wiki/Proprietary_software" title="Proprietary software">proprietary software</a> available at zero price.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2"><span>[</span>3<span>]</span></a></sup> The author usually restricts one or more rights to copy, distribute, and make derivative works of the software.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span>[</span>4<span>]</span></a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Software_license" title="Software license">software license</a> may impose restrictions on the type of use including personal use, individual use, non-profit use, non-commercial use, academic use, commercial use or any combination of these. For instance, the license may be "free for personal, non-commercial use".</p>
<p>Accordingly, freeware may or may not be <a href="/wiki/Free_and_open_source_software" title="Free and open source software">free and open source software</a> and, in order to distinguish, the <a href="/wiki/Free_Software_Foundation" title="Free Software Foundation">Free Software Foundation</a> asks users to avoid calling <a href="/wiki/Free_software" title="Free software">free software</a> as "freeware",<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4"><span>[</span>5<span>]</span></a></sup> the principal difference being that free software can be used, studied, and modified without restriction; free software embodies the concept of freedom to use, while freeware that of free-of-charge. Freeware is also different from <a href="/wiki/Shareware" title="Shareware">shareware</a>; the latter obliges the user to pay after some trial period or to gain additional functionality.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5"><span>[</span>6<span>]</span></a></sup></p>
